### How this changes things for EB-1A India (and EB-1 India broadly)
EB-1A (Extraordinary Ability) falls under the same worldwide + per-country EB-1 cutoff as EB-1B/EB-1C. The single India EB-1 FAD/DOF applies to all of them. No subcategory-specific carve-outs in the Visa Bulletin or the model.

**Immediate / real-world effect (from the actual bulletin data):**
* **Retrogression of ~2 months on FAD**: Previous (2026-06 VB) FAD was `2022-12-15`. Now `2022-10-15`. Anyone with an India EB-1 priority date on/after 2022-10-16 (and before the old cutoff) lost "current" status for Final Action. They cannot have a visa issued or I-485 approved based on this bulletin.
* DOS is using the **Final Action Dates chart** (not Dates for Filing) for all employment-based adjustment of status in July 2026. DOF (`2023-12-01`) is unchanged but irrelevant for most AOS right now.
* Explicit DOS note (Section E of the bulletin): "High demand and number use by aliens chargeable to India in the EB-1 visa category made it necessary to retrogress... Further retrogression or making the category unavailable may be necessary in the coming months if India’s pro-rated limit in the EB-1 category is reached before the fiscal year ends." This is a warning for Jul–Sep 2026.
* Context: This happens even with the model's restriction savings + EB-4/5 spillovers feeding extra numbers into EB-1. India demand (I-140 pipeline + pending inventory, including many EB-1A cases that often have relatively recent PDs) is exceeding the available India slice of the EB-1 pool in the current FY.

**Model / VBPredictor impact (the forecasting engine that powers `/api/vb-forecast`, the VB forecast page, and feeds into OppenheimSolver calibration):**
* `latest_actual` is now `{"bulletin_month": "2026-07", "fad": "2022-10-15", "dof": "2023-12-01"}` (was 2022-12-15).
* The transition that just occurred (2026-06 → 2026-07) is a **-61 day retrogression**. This is now the last entry in `get_advancement_rates()`.
* `get_advancement_stats(recent_n=12)` (the numbers that drive blended forecasts): recent_avg dropped to **+20.2 days/month**, recent_median **0.0**, total retrogression count in the full history **= 5** (n_datapoints=88). Previously the big forward jump into 2026-06 had produced a much stronger recent average.
* `forecast()` (any months_ahead) now:
  * Anchors every future prediction from the earlier `2022-10-15` FAD.
  * Uses the tempered recent rate (~20 d/mo blended 70/30 with seasonal + any supply scaling) for forward movement.
  * Near-term confidence bands and point predictions are therefore more conservative / start from a worse baseline.
  * DOF lead (median gap) is still derived from recent non-C months (remains ~9-10 months in the data).
* Overall: The new data point increases the historical "demand pressure / occasional back-up" signal in the seasonal + advancement model. Forecasts for India EB-1 will now reflect that even after prior rapid movement, the cutoff can (and did) retreat when India usage gets too heavy relative to the pro-rated annual limit.
